# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Three overlapping audiences who each need to feel something specific:
- **Conference organizers** evaluating whether to invite Jenny to speak — need to feel she's credible, range-spanning, and worth a keynote slot
- **Recruiters & hiring managers** for senior technical roles — need to see depth, impact, and rare domain expertise (Edge AI, TinyML, embedded ML)
- **Technical peers and community** (fellow engineers, researchers, DevRel folks) — want to see what she's built, thought about, and where she's pointing
All three need to feel confident in her expertise *and* warm toward her as a person. The portfolio must do both simultaneously.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, curious, accessible.**
Jenny works at the frontier of a young field (Edge AI) and specializes in making hard things understandable. She co-authored an O'Reilly book, speaks at conferences, and bridges embedded hardware with ML. The voice is: expert who doesn't gatekeep, someone who's excited about ideas and wants you to be too.

Not: corporate, cold, technical-flex, overly polished.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference feel**: Design studio / portfolio — distinctive visual identity, conceptual approach (Pentagram, Are.na, Sagmeister). Not generic tech portfolio.
- **Theme**: Light — this is a professional portfolio browsed in daylight, across contexts
- **Typography priority**: The current Inter + Playfair Display pairing is too default. Needs something with more character and warmth.
- **Color**: Pink is the brand color — non-negotiable. Use rose/pink family (hue 345–358, chroma 0.18–0.20) rather than the original corporate hot pink (`#c2185b` / oklch 47% 0.27). Terracotta, amber, and orange-adjacent tones were tried and explicitly rejected as "too brown."
- **Polish priority**: Micro-details, motion, and overall refinement. The structure is basically right; the craft needs elevating.
- **Anti-reference**: Generic tech portfolio with card grids, hero metrics, and gradients. Big rounded icons over section headings. AI-slop aesthetic.

### Design Principles
1. **Credibility through restraint** — don't shout expertise; let the work and writing demonstrate it
2. **Warmth through specificity** — real details (the book cover, exact talks, real companies) beat generic placeholder layout
3. **Curious over polished** — a slight roughness or unexpected choice reads as human and considered, not corporate-smooth
4. **Earned emphasis** — typographic hierarchy should feel intentional; not everything is important
5. **One memorable thing** — each section should have one detail someone will remember (not ten animations competing for attention)
